Supply Structure

One Source, Three Platforms, Global Reach

Liposoma operates from three locations in the Netherlands — R&D and formulation in Amsterdam, production management from Groningen, and goods dispatch from Almere. NutraTeq™ lipid paste is also produced from our Turkey facility for partners where that origin suits their supply or regulatory requirements.

This structure gives us flexibility across order types without fragmenting the quality controls that sit behind every batch. Your ingredient leaves our facility with a consistent documentation package regardless of which platform it comes from or which market it is going to.

Global Logistics

Structured for International Shipment

Our supply relationships span distributors, supplement manufacturers, and nutraceutical brands across Europe, the UK, the US, Australia, and further markets. Shipping liposomal ingredients — particularly temperature-sensitive formats — at international scale requires logistics planning that goes beyond standard freight. We manage that on our side so your procurement team does not have to.

Format-Appropriate Shipping

LipoFlow™ liquid suspensions and NutraTeq™ paste require refrigerated storage conditions. We coordinate shipping with appropriate cold chain requirements built in — not left to the partner to figure out after the order is placed.

Fresh Batch Production

We produce to order on a batch-aligned cycle, which means your ingredient arrives fresh rather than sitting in a warehouse against an uncertain production date. Lead times are communicated clearly before order confirmation so your production schedule can plan accordingly.

Screening Beyond Standard CoA

Our raw material sourcing and production are supported by risk-based contaminant screening that goes beyond standard CoA parameters. This matters for partners operating in markets where regulatory scrutiny on ingredient purity is elevated — particularly EU food supplement and pharmaceutical nutrition categories.

Documentation & Compliance

The Paperwork Your Import Team Needs

Importing nutraceutical ingredients across regulated markets generates a documentation burden that can delay orders and frustrate procurement teams. We treat documentation as part of the supply transaction — not something chased after the shipment has already left.

Issued Per Batch, Every Order

  • Certificate of Analysis (CoA) — active content, purity, physical characteristics
  • Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS) — handling, storage, and safety classification
  • Product specifications sheet — formulation parameters, particle size, carrier composition
  • Residual solvent and contaminant declarations where applicable
  • Halal certificate where relevant to platform and order

Market-Specific Documentation

  • Export documentation aligned with EU, TGA (Australia), and FDA (US) requirements
  • Regulatory file support for market authorisation and product dossier preparation
  • Certificate of Origin on request
  • Technical data package available for partner quality teams conducting ingredient qualification

Documentation Before Commitment

We make documentation available for partner review before an order is placed — not after. If your procurement or regulatory team needs to see CoA data, specification sheets, or certification status ahead of a purchase decision, we can provide that as part of the qualification process.

How We Work

Long-Term Supply Relationships, Not One-Off Transactions

The type of partners we work best with are not looking for a single trial order — they are building a product range or a supply category that requires a consistent, reliable ingredient source across multiple order cycles. That is the relationship model we are built for.

We are transparent about what that looks like in practice: clear communication on volumes, lead times, and specifications before the first order; consistent batch performance across repeat orders; and direct access to our technical and operations teams when questions arise — not a customer service queue.

01

Transparent Lead Times From the Start

We confirm production schedule, batch availability, and estimated dispatch before order confirmation. You get a realistic timeline — not an optimistic estimate that shifts after payment.

02

Direct Technical Access

Questions about specification, stability, regulatory data, or formulation compatibility go directly to our R&D and operations teams. The same people who produced your batch are the ones who can answer questions about it.

03

Smaller Runs Supported Where Relevant

We recognise that not every partner arrives at commercial volume from day one. Initial production runs at smaller scale are supported where the fit is right — including during product development and market testing phases.

04

Volume and Specification Stability

Once a formulation and supply arrangement is established, we maintain specification consistency across subsequent batches. Changes to formulation, carrier composition, or process parameters are communicated in advance — not discovered on receipt.
